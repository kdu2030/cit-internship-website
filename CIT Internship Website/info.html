Common FAQs

What if I'm going on vacation?

If a student's family is going on a planned vacation, it is the student's responsibility to contact his or her employer and inform them of their absence.

What if the student is on vacation during an in-class meeting?

If the student is absent during an in-class meeting, students can use a video call to attend the session. This also applies if a student is absent on the day of final presentations.

If I have already completed my 160 hours before the course ends, do I still have to work?

A student has to complete his or her commitment to his or her employer. Therefore, if you already have worked 160 hours, you still have to continue working until the course ends or your commitment is complete.

Has any student not found an internship?

Historically, every student (that has proactively sought an internship) has found an internship opportunity.

Will the student get paid for his or her internship?

While students might not get paid for their internships, non-paid positions also offer compensation for the student's work. For example, if the student is working for an university, the mentor (who is a college professor) could write a recommendation for the student when he or she is applying for college.

Can my mentor be related to me?

Mentors cannot be related to you. However, you can have a neighbor or a family friend provide an internship for you.

What if I find a new organization willing to host an intern?

If you find a new organization willing to host an intern, you will have to talk to email the internship packet to the mentor so that the organization knows what to expect. In addition, you have to make sure that the work that you're doing for the organization is 75% tech related. Finally, Mrs. Norris will have to speak to the mentor in a in-person meeting.

Can an intern work at home?

An intern CANNOT work at home for extended periods of time. He or she must work on site for his or her internship.

Requirements

  • Minimum of 160 hours worked
  • $150 class fee
  • Internship MUST be approved by Mrs. Norris

Final Assignment

  • Written form of verification of hours
  • Time sheets and weekly reports
  • Final report and presentation

Class Time

  • Online and in-person
  • 30% of grade

Mentor Responsibilities

The mentor should:

  • Know the department sponsoring the internship
  • Guide the intern to make a good impression
  • Suggest challenges the intern should tackle
  • Review evaluation, hours, and final work

Parent Responsibilities

The parent should:

  • Ask your child about their time as an intern
  • Stay involved and know what's going on
  • Ask your child to explain anything you don't understand
  • Make this a learning experience for your child

Student Responsiblities

Attendance

The student should...

  • Be on time
  • Call mentor to inform them of an absence
  • Remember: multiple absences or tardies will cause the student to fail the course

Professional Behavior

  • This will allow you to make connections in the future
  • Make sure you make a good impression of the CIT, Deep Run, and HCPS

Appearance

  • This depends on the internship selected
  • Make sure you're wearing professional attire

Attitude

The student should...

  • ALWAYS display a positive attitude
  • Ask for advice if necessary

Confidentiality

  • The student should respect privacy and confidentiality
  • There are times where an intern cannot be involved

Internship Activites

  • Internship activities should be applicable to skills you learned in the classroom:
    • Project Management
    • Programming
    • Web Design / Development
    • Databases
    • Hardware
    • Software
  • Students should select their internship based on their interests to make sure the work is relevant and enjoyable

Requirements for New Organizations

  • Work done must be 75% IT related
  • Mentor, owner, director, etc. must NOT be a relative
  • Intern must work in a physical workplace environment
  • Internship start date must be no later than June 1, and no later than June 24
  • Internship MUST last during the duration of the Summer Internship Course

Requirements for Mentors

  • Cannot be related
  • Cannot work at home
  • Cannot be an authoritative figure to relative

Discipline

Code of conduct is still applicable to the intern even at the workplace. In addition, if a student is terminated from his or her position for any reason, the student will receive a failing grade and will be kicked out of the CIT.
